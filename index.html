About Me

Welcome to my Epicodus project portfolio. The purpose of this page show off some of the projects I've been working on over the weeks at this program. Additionally, it's a good place to share a little bit about myself and my background with you.

I grew up in the suburbs near Bellevue, WA and I was interested in computers from a young age. Some of my earliest memories consist of working with my dad on managing system memory to get games to work on an old 386-based PC, and I've been hooked ever since. So when my fourth grade class undertook a year-long project of creating computer games, I was beyond excited. This was my first experience with programming, we worked in a language called Logo, a programming language designed as a learning tool. The game I created was called Danger Zone and featured aeronautical trivia and interactive segments where the player navigates planes to their landing strip. After that computers remained a constant hobby throughout my life, whether it was through gaming, assembling PC hardware for myself and friends, or small-scale coding projects.

My career in project management has given me the opportunity to oversee development teams working on a variety of different kinds of applications, and as a result I have developed a very wide but shallow knowledge of most aspects of software development. Because of my interest in computing as a whole, over time I found myself less interested in management and more interested in the coding itself, so it seemed like a logical evolution for me to transition into software development as a profession.
